Daily News Briefing

Iran Protests and Crackdown

Reports indicate ongoing protests in Iran are being met with severe government crackdowns, resulting in numerous gunshot eye injuries in a single hospital. International figures like Donald Trump have warned of 'very strong action' if protesters are executed. Discussions also cover US interference and the impact of these events on the Iranian regime.

Thailand Crane Collapse

At least 22 people have died after a construction crane collapsed onto a passenger train in Thailand.

Kiefer Sutherland Arrest

Actor Kiefer Sutherland was arrested on suspicion of felony criminal threats after an alleged altercation with a ride-hail driver in Los Angeles. He was released on a $50,000 bond, and his first court appearance is scheduled for February 2.

Arizona Groundwater Restrictions

Arizona is implementing significant groundwater restrictions and modernizing water management due to drought. This includes limiting new developments in the Phoenix area, where groundwater supplies are insufficient for the next century, and establishing a new Active Management Area in La Paz County (Ranegras Plain Basin) to curb excessive pumping, particularly by out-of-state agricultural interests. Governor Katie Hobbs has also formed a Water Policy Council to update the state's groundwater laws and address loopholes in rural areas.

Supreme Court on Transgender Athletes

The U.S. Supreme Court is deliberating cases concerning state laws that ban transgender athletes from competing in school sports teams matching their gender identity. Oral arguments were heard on Tuesday, January 13, 2026, for bans in Idaho and West Virginia. With a conservative majority, the Court appears likely to uphold these state bans, impacting legal questions around the Equal Protection Clause and Title IX. A recent poll indicated a majority of U.S. adults favor requiring transgender children to compete on teams matching their sex assigned at birth.
